Developed by the International Gargoub Development Consortium (IDGC), North West Gate Gargoub is a Sustainable, Green & Smart Special Economic Zone and Port on Egypt’s Mediterranean coast, west of Marsa Matrouh. It is designed as a major logistics, trade, and commercial hub, linking Africa, Europe, Asia, and the Americas.

The project includes:
  • 8 km of quays with multipurpose & container terminals for local, regional & international cargo.
  • Logistics & storage facilities (steel, construction materials, grain, oil & gas, tank farms).
  • Business & financial district, multinational HQs, AI & tech hubs, telecom, data centers.
  • Residential, educational, and touristic areas including resorts, entertainment, Marine University, and green smart city neighborhoods.
  • Industrial facilities: textiles, shoes, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, automotive assembly & electric vehicles, shipyard, food processing.
  • Renewable energy projects: hydrogen, solar, and wind plants.

The Alexandria–Gargoub–Salloum Corridor will span over 700 feddans, creating an economic zone comparable to the Suez Canal and Ain Sokhna.
